Keep Scroll example

The keep-scroll directive is used to preserve scroll position when navigating around an Angular app.

Ever clicked a link half-way a very long list, only to find you had to scroll all the way down again when navigating back? This directive fixes this problem by storing the scroll position in window and responding to the $routeChangeStart event.

Usage

<div keep-scroll="myIdentifier"></div>

myIdentifier will be used to store the element's scroll position in window. Whenever the $routeChangeStart event fires the scroll position is corrected.

Waiting for content

When working with asynchronous data, the directive will often be applied too fast, before the data is rendered, and your page is not yet high enough for this to make sense.

In those cases, use the wait-for-event attribute, and emit an event from your controller whenever you're done:

<div keep-scroll="myIdentifier" wait-for-event="contentLoaded"></div>

// from your controller
$scope.$emit('contentLoaded');
